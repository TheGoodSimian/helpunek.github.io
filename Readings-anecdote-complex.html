Complex Anecdotes

Przyjrzyjmy się nieco bardziej złożonej anegdocie. Często, opowiadając historię, musimy podać dodatkowy kontekst lub wyjaśnić porządek chronologiczny. Robimy to, używając czasu past perfect. Podobnie jak w prostych anegdotach, używamy czasu również past simple oraz past continuous.

Several years ago I was in Nepal with a group of friends receiving a series of teachings from a Tibetan Lama. This teacher lived in a monastery at the top of a hill. Each day we would ascend the hill by a trail that passed a few little village shops near the base, then wove back and forth in switchbacks until it reached the monastery perched at its summit. One day we were hurrying up the trail. I turned to notice that two friends had fallen behind. They had stopped at the outskirts of the tiny village and were standing near a sobbing young girl. She had been sent to the shop to purchase two eggs but had accidentally broken one and was afraid to go home for fear her mother would beat her. My friends had stopped and talked with her, and had sent a boy with two rupees to buy another egg.
